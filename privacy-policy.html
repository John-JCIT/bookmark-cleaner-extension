Privacy Policy

Bookmark Cleaner — Last updated: March 2026

Overview

Bookmark Cleaner is a Chrome extension developed by Fair Dinkum Dev. We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains what data the extension accesses and how it is used.

Data We Access

Bookmark Cleaner accesses your Chrome bookmarks (titles, URLs, and folder structure) solely to display, organise, and help you clean them up. This data is read and processed locally in your browser and is never transmitted to any external server or third party.

Dead Link Scanning

When you run the Dead Link Scanner, the extension sends HTTP requests directly from your browser to the bookmark URLs in order to check whether they are still live. These requests are made by your browser to the target websites — no data passes through Fair Dinkum Dev servers. The results are stored temporarily in memory and discarded when you close the extension tab.

Data We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect, store, or transmit your bookmark data.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking of any kind.
  • We do not store any personal information.
  • We do not use cookies.

Permissions

The extension requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • bookmarks — to read, edit, and delete your bookmarks.
  • tabs — to open the extension in a new tab and to send messages between the extension page and the background service worker.
  • host permissions (<all_urls>) — required for the Dead Link Scanner to make HTTP requests to bookmark URLs in order to check if they are still reachable.
